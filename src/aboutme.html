Hello!

Many of the design concepts I care about are based on experiences gained from owning our own small restaurant for 15 years. There were many aspects of our business that peaked my interest in UX and UI, and the importance of data for reporting and decision making. We used a POS (Point of Sale) system to track the goods that sold the most and used the data to make decisions on our menu. We also depended on our website and reviews to bring in new business. These observations started my journey to a formal education in User Experience and Design.

For the last three years I have been studying the principles of User Experience and Design at Weber State University in Ogden Utah and received a certification for this in August of 2022, I am a semester away from my Degree in User Experience and Design.

I have a big family and we enjoy getting together for a family meal every holiday and birthday. We love our little wiener dogs. For many years my husband and I worked on every holiday at our restaurant, but now we are able to cook together at home every night, and the meals are spectacular. My favorite things to do are listening to old records and cooking with my family. I also enjoy Pilates and sew whenever I can.

Prototyping! Prototyping is my favorite step in building apps and/or pages. I have really enjoyed creating mockups on paper and in XD and then testing them out on anyone I can find..

I use it all the time, I find this field applies to most things I do. When starting up a process or project, I try to interview the customer and use the learned methods of User Research to get to the heart of the need and to find an efficient solution.

AI is a useful tool and can help create things faster, or help you when you get stuck. The mechanics of web pages or aps may be able to be completed quickly and efficiently with AI but tackling the right problems and getting to the heart of what can be done to improve things will still need to investigated by humans.

Someone that I have really enjoyed reading and studying is Don Norman. He was one of the founders of the Apple User Experience team. He is also a professor and author. I enjoyed reading his book "The Design of Everyday Things" and now I see "Norman doors" everywhere.
